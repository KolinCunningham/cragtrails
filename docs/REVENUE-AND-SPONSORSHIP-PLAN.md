# CragTrails Revenue & Sponsorship Plan

**Core Principle (non-negotiable):**  
The entire maps, routes, beta, photos, logging, and community features are **100% free forever**. Revenue only subsidizes servers, data quality, and keeping the free experience world-class. No paywalls on core. Ever.

This plan was developed during a multi-agent CEO sprint. The skeptical CEO only approved the most tasteful, opt-in, climber-respecting surfaces after rigorous proof.

## Approved Revenue Streams (Minimal & Ethical)

1. **Tasteful Climber-Brand Partnerships** (Primary long-term)
   - "Partners keeping it free" section with logos and hover notes.
   - Strictly climber/outdoor brands only (safety, education, access focus).
   - No interstitials, no tracking for ads, no data selling.

2. **One-time Offline Packs** ($4.99 per major area or region)
   - Download full maps, photos, beta, and topos for offline use.
   - Core experience always works online for free.
   - One-time purchase (no subscription trap).

3. **GitHub Sponsors + Open Collective**
   - Direct supporter model for individuals and small teams who love the mission.
   - Unlocks all offline packs + nice thank-you badge.

4. **Relevant Gear Affiliates** (very light)
   - "Gear for this route" cards inside route detail (horizontal scroll).
   - Only shown when relevant. Clearly labeled "supports the project".
   - 4–8% typical commission.

## Brand Research (15 High-Affinity Targets)

See the full researched list with contact notes and affinity reasoning in the original agent output. Top priorities:
- Petzl (safety education)
- Black Diamond (skills + access)
- REI Co-op (clinics + distribution)
- Arc'teryx, La Sportiva, Patagonia, Mammut, Edelrid, etc.

## Conservative Year 1 Projection (Approved)
- 4–6 brand deals: $120–180k annualized
- Offline pack sales + GitHub Sponsors: $60–90k
- Light affiliates: $20–40k
- **Total ~$200–310k** (enough to cover serious infra, moderation, and continued free development)

All revenue is reinvested into the free product and open data contributions (OpenBeta, etc.).

## CEO-Approved Proof (Why This Does Not Alienate Users)

> AllTrails proved at massive scale that you can have an incredibly generous free core (world-class maps, photos, logging, community) + tasteful premium (offline) + relevant brand partnerships — and users stay extremely loyal because the free experience is *excellent*.

CragTrails follows the same model, but even stricter:
- Core is better than AllTrails for climbers (routes, grades, beta, sends, conditions).
- Revenue surfaces are smaller, more transparent, and more opt-in.
- We repeatedly say "Core always free" in the UI.
- No dark patterns. No forced accounts for basic use. No selling user data.

This is sustainable without compromising the mission or the 10-year-old simplicity.

## Transparency Commitments
- Public revenue dashboard (in admin, visible to logged-in users eventually).
- Clear "This helps keep maps free" language everywhere.
- No pay-to-log, no pay-to-view-beta, no pay-to-upload-photos.

This plan was only accepted after the skeptical CEO process required clear proof that it strengthens (rather than erodes) the free, simple, 10-year-old-friendly experience.
